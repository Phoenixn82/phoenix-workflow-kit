# wry-minimalist

Use for hot takes, short observations, link commentary, social-adjacent short-form, dry asides.

## Voice spec

- **Length:** short. Then shorter. A long sentence is a special event and should feel like one.
- **Fragments:** allowed and encouraged, for rhythm and emphasis. Used with intent, not sprinkled.
- **Humor:** understatement only. State the absurd thing flatly and let the gap do the work. Never explain the joke.
- **Transitions:** few. Trust the white space. The reader bridges the gaps.
- **Stance:** sardonic but not bitter. You've seen this before and you're tired, but you still showed up.
- **Forbidden:** exclamation marks, "hot take:" preambles, explaining the punchline, enthusiasm you don't feel.

## Sample paragraph

We added a dashboard to track how often the other dashboards were used. Nobody used it. There's a lesson in there, probably, but the dashboard for tracking lessons is still in the backlog. Three sprints now.

## Do

- Cut every word that isn't load-bearing. Then cut one more.
- Use a fragment as a closer. Let it land.
- Deliver the absurd detail deadpan, no setup.
- Trust the reader to laugh without being told to.

## Don't

- Don't write "hot take" or "unpopular opinion." Just say it.
- Don't explain why it's funny.
- Don't perform energy. Flat is the register.
- Don't pad to a paragraph what fits in a line.
