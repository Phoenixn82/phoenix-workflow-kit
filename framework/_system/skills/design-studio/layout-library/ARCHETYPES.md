# Layout Archetype Library — 30 structural frameworks

> Seeded 2026-06-05. This fixes the "every site looks the same" problem.
>
> **The rule:** A website = **STRUCTURE** (one archetype below) × **STYLE** (a brand DNA: color/type/mood).
> Slop and sameness come from reusing the same STRUCTURE and only changing STYLE. So:
>
> 1. **Pick the archetype that fits the brand's job — deliberately.** Don't default.
> 2. **Never reuse the last archetype** for the same client/portfolio. Rotate.
> 3. **Two sites in the same portfolio must use different archetypes.** (the user's example-heritage
>    used #01 Split Marquee — the same bones as `example_client`. That's the failure this prevents.)
> 4. **You may always compose a NOVEL structure** — these 30 are a floor for variety, not a cage.
>
> Each archetype below is brand-agnostic bones. Combine with any palette/type to get a unique site.
> Read the one you're using; don't pre-load all 30.

---

## How to choose
- **Match structure to the content's real shape.** A studio with 4 proof pieces ≠ a 200-SKU shop ≠ a one-idea manifesto. The right bones come from what's actually being shown, not habit.
- **Let the hero type drive it.** The hero is the archetype's fingerprint — if every hero is "headline-left / image-right," every site is the same site.
- **One signature move per site.** Each archetype has a defining device; commit to it, don't dilute it with three others.

---

## A. Hero-defined landing structures

**01 · Split Marquee** — Header; hero = copy on one side, single visual/mark on the other; stacked sections below. *Signature:* the 50/50 hero. *Best for:* fast, conventional, conversion-safe. *Caution:* THE default — overused. Don't reach for it twice. (This is what made example-heritage feel like example_client.)

**02 · Full-Bleed Statement** — No split. One full-viewport hero: a single massive headline + one CTA over a full-bleed color/photo/gradient; everything else scrolls beneath. *Signature:* type-as-hero, edge-to-edge. *Best for:* bold brands with one strong promise.

**03 · Editorial Column** — Narrow single centered column (~640px), big drop-cap or kicker, generous white margins, runs like a longform article. *Signature:* magazine-article restraint. *Best for:* story-led, founder-led, premium-quiet.

**04 · Centered Manifesto** — Everything centered and narrow, oversized type, a creed/belief leads the page before any product. *Signature:* declaration-first. *Best for:* mission/values-driven brands.

**05 · Magazine Cover** — Hero mimics a print cover: masthead, "coverlines" scattered around one dominant image, issue/date stamp. *Signature:* coverlines + masthead. *Best for:* culture, editorial, lifestyle, bold local pride.

**06 · Poster / Broadside** — One big designed poster as the first screen (typographic or illustrated), then minimal supporting blocks. *Signature:* the poster. *Best for:* events, launches, statement brands.

---

## B. Grid & modular structures

**07 · Bento Grid** — The page (or hero) is a grid of varied-size tiles, each a different content morsel (stat, photo, quote, CTA, mini-demo). *Signature:* the bento tile mosaic. *Best for:* multi-feature, "lots to show," modern-tech.

**08 · Quadrant** — Screen split into 4 bold panels, each a pillar/service/audience; click/scroll to expand. *Signature:* the 2×2. *Best for:* 4 clear offerings or audiences.

**09 · Catalog / Product Grid** — Grid-of-cards first; the homepage IS the shelf. *Signature:* shop-shelf density. *Best for:* e-commerce, portfolios with many items, directories.

**10 · Poster Wall** — Dense wall of equal poster/panel tiles (work, posts, products) with a thin header. *Signature:* gallery wall. *Best for:* prolific makers, agencies with volume.

**11 · Logo-Garden Led** — Tiny hero, then immediately a dense proof wall (logos, press, numbers) as the structural centerpiece. *Signature:* proof-first. *Best for:* trust-heavy B2B, "everyone uses us."

---

## C. Motion & scroll-driven structures

**12 · Scroll-Snap Slides** — Full-screen sections that snap like a deck; each is one idea. *Signature:* deck-on-scroll. *Best for:* narrative pitches, products with a sequence.

**13 · Horizontal Scroll** — Primary content scrolls sideways (gallery, timeline, chapters). *Signature:* the sideways move. *Best for:* portfolios, process stories, galleries.

**14 · Sticky-Stack Reveal** — Sections pin and stack/overlap as you scroll, each revealing over the last. *Signature:* layered reveal. *Best for:* premium product storytelling.

**15 · Timeline / Path** — A vertical or winding line is the page's spine; content hangs off milestones. *Signature:* the path. *Best for:* process, history, journeys, "how it works."

**16 · Marquee-Spine** — Bold scrolling marquees are the structural connective tissue between blocks. *Signature:* recurring tickers. *Best for:* loud, energetic, retail/sport brands.

---

## D. Navigation-defined structures

**17 · Sidebar Rail** — Fixed vertical rail (brand + nav + contact) on one edge; content scrolls in the remaining column. *Signature:* the persistent rail. *Best for:* studios, docs, app-like marketing.

**18 · Split-Screen Persistent** — Two fixed half-screens; one holds a static statement/visual, the other scrolls. *Signature:* one-side-still. *Best for:* two-part stories, duos, before/after.

**19 · Off-Canvas Drawer** — Stark minimal page; nav and deeper content slide in as drawers/overlays. *Signature:* hidden-until-summoned. *Best for:* minimalist, fashion, single-focus.

**20 · Hub & Spoke** — A central hub element (orb, map, menu) with sections radiating from it. *Signature:* the center. *Best for:* multi-service where the brand is the connector.

**21 · Index / Directory** — Typographic list/table as the homepage: numbered rows, hover-expands. *Signature:* the line-item index. *Best for:* editorial, utilitarian-cool, large catalogs of ideas.

---

## E. Object & metaphor structures

**22 · Letter / Note** — The page is designed as a personal letter or handwritten note from the founder. *Signature:* the letter. *Best for:* intimate, founder-led, trust-by-warmth.

**23 · Zine / Cut-Paste** — DIY zine: mixed columns, pasted clippings, varied type, intentional roughness. *Signature:* cut-and-paste energy. *Best for:* indie, youth, music, scrappy local.

**24 · Receipt / Ticket** — Structured like a receipt/ticket/stub: monospace, dotted rules, line items. *Signature:* the paper artifact. *Best for:* pricing-led, food, events, playful utility.

**25 · Dashboard Hero** — Hero shows a live (or live-looking) product UI/dashboard doing the thing. *Signature:* product-as-hero. *Best for:* SaaS, tools, "watch it work" proof.

**26 · Tri-Fold / Triptych** — Three vertical panels like an opened brochure or altarpiece. *Signature:* three columns of equal weight. *Best for:* 3 pillars, comparison, balanced storytelling.

**27 · Map / Place** — A map or place is the organizing structure; content is pinned to locations. *Signature:* geography. *Best for:* local, multi-location, travel, real estate.

---

## F. Expressive / anti-template structures

**28 · Brutalist Stack** — Raw full-width stacked blocks, heavy borders, system/mono type, zero rounding, exposed grid. *Signature:* unpolished honesty. *Best for:* counter-culture, art, dev tools, bold contrarians.

**29 · Asymmetric Collage** — Overlapping cards, photos, notes, and marks placed off-grid at slight angles. *Signature:* curated chaos. *Best for:* creative studios, scrapbook warmth, personality-forward.

**30 · Diagonal / Skew** — Slanted dividers and skewed color blocks drive the whole composition; nothing sits on a flat horizontal. *Signature:* the diagonal energy. *Best for:* sport, motion, bold/fast brands.
